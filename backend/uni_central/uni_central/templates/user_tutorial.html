User Tutorial

Get started with UniCentral in under 5 minutes! This quick guide will help you make the most of our platform's features.

  • Getting Started
  • Finding Courses
  • Course Reviews
  • Study Buddies
  • Course Planner

1. Getting Started

Create Your Account

Sign up with your university email to access all features of UniCentral.

  1. Click "Register" in the top navigation bar
  2. Enter your email address
  3. Fill in your name and set a password

2. Finding Courses

Browse & Filter Courses

UniCentral makes it easy to find the perfect courses for your needs.

  1. Navigate to the "Courses" page from the main menu
  2. Browse courses by department or use the search function
  3. Apply filters to narrow down results by difficulty, rating, or credits
  4. Click on any course to view detailed information

3. Course Reviews

Read & Write Course Reviews

Get insights from peers and share your own experiences.

  1. On any course page, scroll down to see reviews
  2. Click "Write a Review" to share your experience
  3. Rate the course on difficulty, workload, and overall quality
  4. Add detailed comments to help other students
  5. Optionally submit anonymously for privacy

4. Study Buddies

Connect with Classmates

Find and message classmates to form study groups or get help.

  1. Navigate to "My Classmates" from your dashboard
  2. Browse students in your enrolled courses
  3. Click "Connect" to send a study buddy request
  4. Once connected, exchange messages through the built-in chat

5. Course Planner

Plan Your Academic Journey

Map out your entire degree program with our semester planner.

  1. Go to "Course Planner" from your dashboard
  2. Create semesters by clicking "Add Semester"
  3. Search for courses and add them into your plan
  4. The planner will show credit totals
  5. Optionally upload your transcript to import past courses

6. Discussion Boards

Participate in Course Discussions

Ask questions and share insights with the entire class community.

  1. From any course page, click on the "Discussions" tab
  2. Browse existing threads or create a new topic
  3. Reply to questions from other students
